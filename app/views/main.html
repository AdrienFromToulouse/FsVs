The emergence
in Durban

Fruits and Veggies, Durban's pre-emiment gypsy ska trouble-makers emerged from the gutters of Umbilo in 2008 and have since earned themselves a reputation as one of the most refreshing and powerful live acts in the country.

The band's
members

Lead singer Purity’s powerful and wild vocals are backed by a band full of raw guts and balls; comprising guitarist Jimbo, bassist Loopy, drummer Justin and guitarist Cameron.

Mahala
called them

“a one of a kind band, solid songs, a derisive attitude towards conventions, a blistering stage presence and a really deep commitment to living life to the full. The most exciting post-racial, post-punk party band to come out of Durban since, uh, forever!”

Reviewing their
Splashy Fen set

Lloyd Gedye wrote in the Mail and Guardian: “the most impressive of the bands was Fruits and Veggies... teenage rebellion Durban style, with some great catchy hooks and some fierce attitude.”

Fruits and Veggies
have an EP

“Ndaa” under their belt , they have recently been the subject of a Rolling Stone feature and have just finished the demo process of their first full length album at Sonic Studios with Colin Peddie.

Levi's Music Mag
called them

“pure genre-breaking liberation” and wrote: "Fruit and Veg are skanky, Fruit and Veg are free-range... with ska elements in a mix of punk and funk. Fruit and Veg are a band for a big stage. They are loose without being loose, tight without being uptight".
